Here is a quantitative research plan for the v15 fixed-budget feature-replacement cycle.

### **Section 1: Executive Summary**

* **The Problem with v14's Baseline Failure:** The ensemble\_ridge\_gbt model improved on the live stack but failed to meaningfully beat the historical-mean baseline. This usually implies the model is fitting to short-term noise rather than capturing the persistent structural drivers of the benchmark universe over a 6-month horizon.  
* **Weaknesses in the Current Set:** The current feature\_engineering.py relies on generic, short-term price technicals (rsi\_14, macd\_hist, bb\_pct\_b, sma\_12m) which have near-zero predictive power for 6-month horizons. Furthermore, nominal dollar features like underwriting\_income scale with time, destroying Ridge regression weights.  
* **PGR Feature Redundancy:** The current pipeline has overlapping underwriting signals (combined\_ratio\_ttm, pif\_growth\_yoy, cr\_acceleration, npw\_growth\_yoy). We need to consolidate these into *spreads* and *ratios* that provide orthogonal information.  
* **The Missing Benchmark Link:** The current features are highly PGR-myopic. Predicting relative returns for VWO, VXUS, GLD, and BND requires pricing the macro regime. Specifically, we are missing US Dollar momentum (critical for EM/Gold) and Equity Risk Premium (critical for VOO vs BND).  
* **v15 Strategy:** Rip out 1990s-style technical indicators and redundant/nominal PGR metrics. Replace them one-for-one with economically grounded macro spreads, relative valuation ratios, and pricing-power indicators sourced from free FRED/EDGAR data.

**Section 3: Best Fixed-Budget Replacement Ideas**

**1\. Swap: Technicals for Macro Regimes**

* **Remove:** rsi\_14, macd\_hist  
* **Add:** usd\_momentum\_6m, real\_yield\_change\_6m  
* **Why:** RSI/MACD are mean-reverting oscillators built for days/weeks. At 6 months, relative returns are driven by the Dollar and Real Rates.  
* **Model:** Both (Ridge needs the macro regime for intercepts; GBT splits on it).

**2\. Swap: Nominal Scale for Yield**

* **Remove:** underwriting\_income (nominal $)  
* **Add:** pgr\_fcf\_yield (or underwriting\_margin\_pct if FCF is hard)  
* **Why:** Nominal dollars grow with time, ruining linear models (Ridge). Yield is stationary.  
* **Model:** Primarily Ridge.

**3\. Swap: Collinear Credit for Risk Ratios**

* **Remove:** credit\_spread\_ig, credit\_spread\_hy  
* **Add:** credit\_spread\_ratio (HY/IG), equity\_risk\_premium  
* **Why:** HY and IG are highly collinear (bad for Ridge). A ratio gives a single risk-appetite vector, opening a slot for ERP to price VOO.  
* **Model:** Ridge (removes collinearity).

**4\. Swap: Absolute Margins for Relative Advantage**

* **Remove:** combined\_ratio\_ttm  
* **Add:** pgr\_cr\_vs\_peer\_cr  
* **Why:** If the whole industry faces inflation, PGR's absolute CR looks bad, but if peers are worse, PGR relative returns go *up*.  
* **Model:** GBT (great for isolating alpha vs beta).

**5\. Swap: Redundant Auto Inflation**

* **Remove:** insurance\_cpi\_mom3m  
* **Add:** auto\_pricing\_power\_spread (PPI YoY \- CPI YoY)  
* **Why:** The file currently has both CPI and PPI. The *spread* between wholesale pricing power and retail prices dictates future margin expansion.  
* **Model:** Both.

**6\. Swap: Redundant Volatility**

* **Remove:** vol\_21d  
* **Add:** breakeven\_momentum\_3m  
* **Why:** 21-day vol is too fast for a 6-month forecast. Breakeven momentum provides a missing fundamental driver for DBC and GLD.  
* **Model:** GBT.

**7\. Swap: Absolute PE for Relative PE**

* **Remove:** pe\_ratio  
* **Add:** pgr\_pe\_vs\_market\_pe  
* **Why:** PGR's PE falls when the whole market falls. Relative PE isolates whether PGR is historically cheap *compared to alternative allocations*.  
* **Model:** Ridge.

**8\. Swap: Redundant Momentum**

* **Remove:** mom\_3m  
* **Add:** gold\_vs\_treasury\_6m (GLD \- BND)  
* **Why:** You already have 6m, 12m, and 52w high. Swap the 3m window for a cross-asset fear indicator to help the model predict sudden regime shifts.  
* **Model:** Both.

**9\. Swap: Trend following for Fundamental Lead**

* **Remove:** sma\_12m  
* **Add:** vmt\_gas\_proxy\_yoy  
* **Why:** SMA is purely backward-looking. Gas prices fundamentally predict driving frequency (PGR claims) and price the energy sector (VDE).  
* **Model:** GBT.

**10\. Swap: Complex Curve for Classic Curve**

* **Remove:** yield\_curvature  
* **Add:** term\_premium\_proxy (10Y \- 3M)  
* **Why:** Curvature is often over-engineered. 10Y-3M is the most economically verified predictor of broad benchmark regime shifts.  
* **Model:** Ridge.

**Section 4: Model-Specific Recommendations**

**Top Replacement Features for Ridge (Linear / Stationary Focus):**

1. auto\_pricing\_power\_spread (Linear relationship to future earnings).  
2. equity\_risk\_premium (Stationary valuation metric).  
3. credit\_spread\_ratio (Eliminates HY/IG collinearity).  
4. pgr\_pe\_vs\_market\_pe (Normalizes valuation against broader index bloat).

**Top Replacement Features for GBT (Non-linear / Regime Switch Focus):**

1. usd\_momentum\_6m (Excellent for tree splits to isolate EM/Gold regimes).  
2. pgr\_cr\_vs\_peer\_cr (Trees can identify the threshold where PGR's advantage accelerates).  
3. real\_yield\_change\_6m (Creates clear branching paths for Fixed Income vs Equity).  
4. gold\_vs\_treasury\_6m (Trees use this as a "panic" switch).

**Section 5: Benchmark-Predictive Features**

Currently, the feature matrix assumes PGR features will somehow predict VWO or BND. We *must* include benchmark-predictive features to accurately forecast relative returns.

* **For Broad US Equity (VOO):** equity\_risk\_premium (SP500 EY \- 10Y). Determines the fundamental baseline of the market.  
* **For International/EM (VXUS, VWO):** usd\_momentum\_6m. Emerging markets are inversely correlated with the dollar.  
* **For Fixed Income (BND, VMBS):** real\_yield\_change\_6m. Rate levels matter less than the trajectory of real rates.  
* **For Commodities/Real Assets (GLD, DBC, VDE):** breakeven\_momentum\_3m. DBC and GLD are the model's inflation hedges; it needs to know when inflation expectations are rising.

**Section 6: Data Feasibility**

**Highly Feasible (Use immediately in v15):**

* *FRED Derived (Low effort):* usd\_momentum\_6m (DTWEXBGS), real\_yield\_change\_6m (DFII10), credit\_spread\_ratio (BAMLH0A0HYM2 / BAA10Y), breakeven\_momentum\_3m (T10YIE), vmt\_gas\_proxy\_yoy (GASREGCOVW), term\_premium\_proxy (T10Y3M).  
* *Price DB Derived (Zero cost):* gold\_vs\_treasury\_6m (GLD/BND prices).  
* *DB Derived (Low effort):* auto\_pricing\_power\_spread (PPI \- CPI, already partially in DB).

**Medium Feasibility (Requires slight pipeline updates):**

* *EDGAR / Peer DB:* pgr\_cr\_vs\_peer\_cr (Requires peer fundamentals to be parsed alongside PGR).  
* *FMP:* equity\_risk\_premium and pgr\_pe\_vs\_market\_pe (Requires fetching VOO/SPY fundamentals from FMP, which is supported by their API but needs a new loader).

**Avoid / High Risk:**

* pgr\_buyback\_yield\_vs\_market (Sourcing reliable, point-in-time aggregate SP500 buyback yields is notoriously difficult and requires paid data like S\&P Dow Jones Indices).
